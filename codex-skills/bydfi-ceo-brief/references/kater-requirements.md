# Kater Requirements

Use this file when judging whether the current management brief satisfies Kater's asks.

## Core Content Requirements

- Every key meeting record should form a conclusion, identify department issues, distinguish what is working vs not working, and give executable suggestions.
- Weekly report analysis should connect historical weekly reports with the current week, then point out department issues and personal execution signals.
- CEX progress should be assessed across all available signals, not from one source in isolation.
- MoonX can be treated as a standalone line when its evidence is thick enough to stand on its own.

## Process And Timeliness Requirements

- The daily report should arrive before the management meeting, not after.
- Data should be ingested before analysis; do not generate polished conclusions from incomplete storage.
- Automatic daily updating must be stable, not one-off.

## Quality Requirements

- Reduce hallucinations on key facts.
- Manually verify important points before syncing to management.
- Keep translation and analysis as split stages when the workflow requires traceability.
- Preserve enough internal traceability for audit, but remove that traceability from the management-facing PDF.

## Practical Scoring Rule

Use three buckets in your answer:

- `已满足`: clearly present in the current management brief.
- `部分满足`: visible in content, but incomplete in process, cadence, or consistency.
- `未满足`: absent, unverified, or contradicted by the current evidence boundary.

Do not collapse these into one vague answer.
